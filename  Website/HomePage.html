Gwadar- The gateway of South Asia

Introduction

Gwadar, located in Balochistan province of Pakistan, is a rapidly emerging tourist destination known for its rich natural beauty. From historical landmarks to pristine beaches, this coastal region offers a perfect blend for tourists looking for diverse range of beauty. Its golden beaches and turquoise waters give a breath-taking view which very less people are familiar with. Here's a detailed guide for all Gwadar has to offer.

Amazing facts about Gwadar

  • The word Gwadar comes from two Balochi words translating to 'The gateway of wind'
  • Gwadar is a multi-zone climate region which has deserts, coastal areas, and hilly terrains all close to each other
  • Gwadar is known for its unique landscape. It has a hammerhead-shaped peninsula, where the city lies between two hills

Tourist Attractions in Gwadar

1. Kund Malir Beach

Kund Malir is one of the most scenic beaches of gwardar. From views of the Arabian sea and high mountains, the golden sands and crystal-clear water is an ideal place for photography and picnics.

2. Astola Island (also known as the Island of Seven Hills)

This island, which is accessible by boat, is the biggest offshore island of Pakistan. It offers wide range of activities such as,

  • Snorkelling
  • Camping
  • Bird watching
  • Stargazing

3. Hingol National Park

This is Pakistas largest national park which has an area of a whooping 6000 sq.km. The fact that makes it so special is that it is home for some of the rarest animal species in the world which includes,

  • Ibex
  • Marsh crocodiles
  • Golden jackals
  • Flamingos

4. Koh-e-Batil

This hill lies in the southern edge of gwardar city. It consists of a up trek of 700 step staircase which rewards with the breathtaking views of Arabian sea, and the city coastline. At the top, there is also a hotel for staying at night.
