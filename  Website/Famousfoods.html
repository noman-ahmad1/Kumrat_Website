Famous Foods of Gwadar

When visiting a new place, your tour is incomplete without trying the local food over there. Gwadar's cuisine is a mix of Balochi, Makrani, and Omani influence. This cuisine has some of the most unique food you will ever taste with enriched local spices further enhancing your experience. Here is a list of some of the famous dishes you must try in Gwadar:

1. Balochi Sajji

It is a whole lamb seasoned with salt and mild spices which is placed on skewers and slow cooked underground above coals. This is the signature dish of Balochistan and a dream for meat lovers. It has a smoky and tender texture.

2. Seafood (Fish, Shrimp, Lobster)

When in Gwadar, you can taste the freshest seafood which is often caught literally minutes before it is cooked for you. There is a variety of Pomfret, Red Snapper, Mackerel, Kingfish, and Lobster. This seafood is grilled over charcoal with lemon and spices on it

3. Makai Roti & Saag

This is a very simple and nutritious meal which is slow cooked spinach with spices over tortilla made of cornmeal.

4. Kahwa(Balochi Green Tea)

This is a local staple which has a very light taste of hot water infused with tea leaves and some spices which is ideal for digestion and makes you feel light instantly.

5. Date-based Sweets

Since Balochistan grows a huge variety of high-quality dates, you can find a lot of sweets made from dates like, date cakes, date halwa, and stuffed dates.

Travel Tips: Things to Keep in Mind

Planning ahead of your tour to Gwadar can be very much useful and make your experience more delightful, smooth and fulfilling Here are some useful tips to keep in mind:

  1. Travel Permits: Foreign tourists may require a No objection certificate(NOC) while visiting Gwadar unlike citizens who can travel freely
  2. Road Travel: Makran coastal highway is well maintained and smooth, but carrying extra fuel, snacks and water can save you in case of emergency
  3. Connectivity: The efficiency or mobile signals can vary in patchy areas due to lack of network infrastructure
  4. Clothing: Local customs and traditions must be respected when it comes to clothing especially when visiting rural areas
  5. Cash is King: ATMs may not be available in remote areas so carry enough cash while travelling
  6. Language: Languages spoken locally are Urdu and Balochi so most of the times people won‚Äôt understand English. Use of google translator can ease communication
  7. Accommodation: To avoid any problems, always book in advance whether you want to stay in a luxury or standard hotel. During holidays, hotels running out of rooms is very common.
